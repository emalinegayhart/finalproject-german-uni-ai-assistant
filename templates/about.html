About German Higher Education

Your comprehensive guide to studying in Germany

Types of Universities

Universities (Universität)

Traditional research-oriented institutions offering a wide range of academic programs. Focus on theoretical and research-based education.

Universities of Applied Sciences (Fachhochschule)

Practice-oriented institutions focusing on professional education with strong industry connections and practical training.

Colleges of Art, Film and Music

Specialized institutions focusing on artistic and creative education.

Admission Requirements

General Requirements

  • Higher education entrance qualification (Hochschulzugangsberechtigung)
  • Proof of language proficiency (German and/or English)
  • Previous academic records
  • Motivation letter (for some programs)

Language Requirements

  • German programs: TestDaF or DSH certificate (usually B2/C1 level)
  • English programs: IELTS or TOEFL certificate

Cost of Studying

Tuition Fees

Most public universities in Germany charge no or very low tuition fees. Students typically only pay semester fees (100-350€) covering administrative costs and public transportation.

Living Costs

  • Rent: 300-800€/month
  • Food: 200-300€/month
  • Health insurance: 110€/month
  • Study materials: 50€/month
  • Entertainment/leisure: 100-200€/month

Visa Information

Non-EU students need to apply for a student visa. Requirements typically include:

  • Acceptance letter from a German university
  • Proof of financial resources (blocked account with ~11,000€)
  • Health insurance
  • Valid passport
  • Language certificates
